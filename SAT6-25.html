The Desert's Secret

The sun beat down on the dry, cracked earth of the desert. A lone lizard scurried under a large rock, seeking refuge from the intense midday heat. At first glance, the landscape looked completely empty. However, despite the harsh conditions, the desert was actually teeming with hidden life. Cacti stored precious water in their thick, waxy stems, while nocturnal animals slept deep underground, waiting patiently for the cool night to begin their hunt.

1. What does the word "refuge" mean as it is used in the passage?

2. What is the main idea of this short passage?

3. According to the text, why do nocturnal animals wait for the night?

4. Based on the passage, how do cacti survive in the desert?
